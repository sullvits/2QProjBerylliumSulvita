Intro

This webpage will tackle forms of bias. "The Good" contains examples where bias is intended and necessary. Often, the reader is informed that the piece is not unbiased. "The Bad" contains examples where bias is unacceptable.
Effects of Bias

Bias can be a hindrance to truth. When raw truth becomes tainted by the political opinions of the reporter, the memories of the historian, or the worldview of the experimenter, truth is tarnished. And how can we go about our daily lives with the knowledge of a false truth?

In 1887, Jose Rizal published Noli Me Tangere. His words touched the hearts of aching Filipinos and spurred them to break free from Spanish colonial rule. The Spaniards executed Rizal for his words of truth in 1896. Two world wars and a century later, at a time where ideas move at the speed of light, the power of words has been defiled. By deliberately twisting facts or carelessly letting opinions seep into fact, wrong ideas are propagated. It is up to us readers to stay vigilant and critical, to guard our thoughts, which spur our actions and impact our lives.
